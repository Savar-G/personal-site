### Transform self-love into empathy

Pay deeper attention to people. Attune yourself to the shifting moods of individuals, get a read on what motivates them, and take their perspective — enter their world and value system rather than projecting your own.

### See through people's masks

Don't be naive and mistake appearances for reality; become a master decoder of true feelings. Open your senses and read people on the physical level. Notice cues on the face and the micro-expressions first, then the voice — its pitch and pace — then posture, hands, and the positioning of the legs.

When someone is genuinely engaged, the pitch of their voice rises, the tone is warm and natural, hesitation disappears, and the banter quickens. As comfort grows they lean in, arms unfolded, exchanging more looks — until you see synchrony, the unconscious mirroring of your gestures.

Confidence shows as relaxation: freer movement, more eye contact, fewer nervous smiles, a greater sense of taking up space.

### The law of compulsive behavior

The primary corollary: people never do something just once. They'll excuse it — say they lost their head in the moment — but character and habit make them repeat it. Judge people by the pattern, not the apology.
