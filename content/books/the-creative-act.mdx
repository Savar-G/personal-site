### Ideas pass through you

If you have an idea you're excited about and you don't bring it to life, it's not uncommon for that idea to find its voice through another maker. Not because they stole it — because the idea's time has come.

We all start as natural receivers. Children meet new information with delight instead of comparing it to what they already believe; they live in the moment, are spontaneous more than analytical, curious not jaded. Even the most ordinary experience is met with a sense of awe, with no facade and no attachment to a story. The work is to get back there.

### Discipline and freedom are partners

The more set in your personal regimen, the more freedom you have within the structure to express yourself. Discipline is not a lack of freedom — it's a harmonious relationship with time. Managing your schedule and daily habits well is what frees up the practical and creative capacity to make great art.

### Don't trash the whole for one flaw

Don't abandon a project because you've given in to all-or-nothing thinking. It's easy to see one flaw and want to discard the entire work — that reflex shows up everywhere in life.

Practice truly seeing what's there without a negativity bias. Be open to both strength and weakness. Often 80% of the work is quite good, and if the other 20% fits in just the right way, it becomes magnificent — far better than trashing it because one small part isn't a perfect fit. The inner critic that says "you're not talented enough" is usually an outer voice absorbed earlier in life — a parent, teacher, mentor — not your own. The first step of clearing is acknowledgement.

### The coin toss method

We can only assess something in relation to something else, so when you're deciding between options, keep A/B tests to two choices — any more clouds the process. At a true impasse, try this: decide which option is heads and which is tails, then flip the coin. While the coin is still spinning in the air, notice the quiet preference for one outcome. The one you're rooting for is the one to go with. The test is over before the coin ever lands — it's the one the heart desires.

### Creativity is a way of being

The artist's job is never truly finished. In most occupations, you leave the work behind at the office; the artist is always on call. The job is two kinds — the work of *doing* and the work of *being*. Creativity is something you are, not only something you do. A way of moving through the world every minute, every day.
