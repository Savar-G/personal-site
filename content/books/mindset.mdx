### Two mindsets

Picture being called to the front of a language class and quizzed. In a fixed mindset your ability is on the line and the tension spikes. In a growth mindset you're a novice — that's why you're here — and the teacher is a resource. Same moment, opposite experience. The message of the book: you can change your mindset.

A seventh-grader summed it up — when she's unsure of an answer she raises her hand anyway, because being corrected, or asking "can you help me?", is how she gets smarter. Confront a challenge and make progress over time, and that's how you build a growth mindset.
