### What wealth actually is

Being able to do anything you want, whenever you want — freedom, family, and health. Money is the tool, not the destination.

### Decisions

- **Worst-case consequence analysis:** What's the worst outcome of this choice, and is it life-ending? What's the probability? Is it an acceptable risk?
- Weigh big decisions with a weighted-average matrix rather than gut feel.

### Time

Fastlaners are frugal with time, not pennies. Don't be an "inconvenient saver" — three layovers to save $100 trades your scarcest asset for your most replaceable one. Avoid spending on instant gratification.

### Entry and needs

- The Law of Effection: impact is the multiplier — to make millions, you must affect millions of lives.
- Mind the entry. "Don't dig for gold, sell shovels." When *everyone* is doing something, the edge is gone.
- Stop chasing money; chase needs — problems, pain points, service gaps, emotions. Never start a business for selfish reasons; being an expert in something doesn't make it a good business.
